{} about me

I've been an artist all my life. In fact I often joke that, at heart, I'm still a just five year old who loves drawing dinosaurs. Add to that the fact that I am totally a computer nerd and it would seem that web development may be an ideal career choice for me.

I love the internet. More specifically, I really love when I've finished working on a page, upload it and there it is on the small screen, in all its glory. Perhaps after trouble shooting a bug for twenty minutes and, at long last, getting to the bottom of the problem. Which is gratifying in itself.

The internet was not yet a thing while I was growing up. If you were a designer and your work showed up on the screen it meant it was being broadcast on television, which was a pretty big deal. I think those connotations must have transferred over to seeing my work published on the internet. In any case, I do get juice from it.

My Journey to Becoming a Web Developer

I suppose my journey to web development began the first time I ever saw a 24-bit monitor. Prior to the advent of 24-bit displays, computer monitors were limited to 8-bit displays. The evolution of monitors from 8-bit displays to 24-bit increased the available color palette from 256 to 16,777,216!

This was a quantum leap forward in fidelity. It allowed for fully continuous tone images to be created and displayed. No more blocky color transitions. Anti-aliasing was now possible, eliminating jagged lines drawn on a diagonal. I was sold then and there. I knew that I wanted to pursue a career in computer graphics.

I eventually decided to move into web-development as this field benefits from both my left brain and right brain aptitudes. I took advantage of Amazon's Career Choice program which pays for courses at approved schools. I enrolled in a web development boot camp and feel good in taking a huge step on my journey. To be continued.
